# Product Guidelines: Imputador

## Tone and Voice

- **Concise & Minimalist:** Use the minimum number of words necessary to convey meaning. Avoid technical jargon unless essential for task clarity.
- **Direct & Action-Oriented:** Use active voice and imperative verbs (e.g., "Add Task," "Save Changes") to guide users efficiently.

## Visual Identity

- **Modern & Playful:** A vibrant color palette that brings energy to the workday. Use rounded corners, subtle shadows, and smooth transitions to create a responsive and friendly feel.
- **Data Clarity:** Despite the playful aesthetic, ensure high contrast and clear typography to maintain readability of time-tracking data.

## UX Principles

- **Keyboard-First / Low Friction:** Design for speed. Every core action (logging a task, switching days, editing duration) should be accessible via keyboard shortcuts or minimal clicks.
- **Immediate Feedback:** Provide visual or haptic cues immediately after a user action (e.g., color changes, loading states, success toasts) to confirm the system has processed the input.
- **Information Hierarchy:** Use Material Design elevations and spacing to distinguish between primary actions (adding tasks) and secondary information (historical reports).

## Design System

- **Standard Material Design:** Adhere to Google's Material Design 3 (M3) specifications for components, typography, and iconography to ensure a familiar and accessible user interface.
- **Component Consistency:** Reuse standard Material components (Buttons, Chips, Text Fields, Date Pickers) to maintain a cohesive look and feel throughout the application.
